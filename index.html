Beginner's Guide to Trading

What is Trading?

Trading involves buying and selling financial instruments (like stocks, forex, or cryptocurrencies) with the goal of making a profit from price movements.

Basic Concepts:

  • Market: Where buyers and sellers exchange assets
  • Bid/Ask: The price buyers will pay (bid) vs. what sellers want (ask)
  • Spread: Difference between bid and ask prices
  • Liquidity: How easily an asset can be bought/sold
  • Volatility: How much prices fluctuate over time

Algorithmic Trading Basics:

Algorithmic trading uses computer programs to execute trades based on predefined rules and strategies. Benefits include:

  • Removes emotional decision making
  • Executes trades at optimal speeds
  • Can analyze multiple markets simultaneously
  • Backtests strategies using historical data

Chart Pattern Strategies

Common Chart Patterns:

1. Head and Shoulders

A reversal pattern that signals a potential trend change. Features three peaks - the middle one (head) is highest, flanked by two lower peaks (shoulders).

Strategy: Enter short position when price breaks below the "neckline" with stop-loss above the right shoulder.

2. Double Top/Bottom

Double Top (bearish reversal) has two peaks at similar highs. Double Bottom (bullish reversal) has two troughs at similar lows.

Strategy: For Double Top, sell when price breaks below the support level between peaks. For Double Bottom, buy when price breaks above resistance.

3. Triangles (Ascending, Descending, Symmetrical)

Consolidation patterns where price makes lower highs and higher lows (symmetrical), just lower highs (descending), or just higher lows (ascending).

Strategy: Trade the breakout in direction of the prevailing trend with stop-loss on the other side of the triangle.

4. Flags and Pennants

Short-term continuation patterns after strong moves. Flags are small parallelograms, pennants are small symmetrical triangles.

Strategy: Enter in direction of original trend when price breaks out of pattern, targeting move equal to initial "flagpole".
